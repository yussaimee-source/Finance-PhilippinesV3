Bankers Association of the Philippines

The Bankers Association of the Philippines (BAP) is the lead organisation of universal and commercial banks in the Philippines, consisting of forty-four (44) member banks — twenty (20) local banks and twenty-four (24) foreign bank branches.

Established on March 29, 1949, the BAP frames rules and regulations in cooperation with the Central Bank to increase the efficiency and effectiveness of banking services to the community, and works with legislative, executive and regulatory bodies on laws relating to banks and banking.

Officially incorporated as an SEC-registered entity on August 24, 1964, the BAP continues its mission to ensure public trust and push for policies that improve and protect the welfare of the entire Philippine banking industry and its stakeholders.

The Chamber of Thrift Banks (CTB)

The umbrella organisation of the country's thrift banks. Organised in 1974, CTB has 36 member-banks with over 3,000 branches nationwide, promoting and strengthening the role of thrift institutions in the Philippine economy.
