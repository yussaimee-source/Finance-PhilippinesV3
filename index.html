About the Conference

Shaping the Future of Philippine Banking

The Philippine economy enters 2026 on a measured but resilient footing. The World Bank and ADB project GDP growth in the 5.8–6.1% range, supported by household consumption, election-year fiscal stimulus, infrastructure rollout, and continued OFW remittance inflows of around USD 38 billion.

The Philippine banking sector sits at the intersection of three large transitions: an AI transition moving from co-pilots to autonomous workflows; a money transition in which BSP's Project Agila wholesale CBDC and tokenised securities are reframing what "bank money" means; and a connectivity transition anchored by ASEAN cross-border QR and BSP's Open Finance Phase 2 framework.

Finance Philippines 2026 brings together senior leaders, regulators, and infrastructure providers writing the operating playbook for this new era — biased toward execution: what works, at what unit economics, with what controls.

Keynote

Ana Maria Aboitiz Delgado

President & CEO, UnionBank of the Philippines
President, Bankers Association of the Philippines 2026–2027

Ana Maria Aboitiz-Delgado is the President and Chief Executive Officer of Union Bank of the Philippines, a role she assumed in January 2025 following more than two decades at the institution. She began her career at UnionBank as a management trainee in 2003, rising through roles in SME banking, retail and institutional finance, customer experience, and digital channels.

In March 2026 she was elected President of the Bankers Association of the Philippines (BAP) for the 2026–2027 term — the first woman to lead the organisation in its nearly 80-year history. She succeeded Jose Teodoro K. Limcaoco of BPI.

Aboitiz-Delgado holds a bachelor's degree from Boston College and an MBA from New York University's Stern School of Business (2010). Prior to UnionBank she was at Citibank NA in product management.

In 2025 she was named one of Fortune magazine's 100 Most Powerful Women in Asia (ranked 94th) — the youngest Filipino and only first-time Philippine honouree. She has guided UnionBank's digital transformation including the acquisition of Citibank NA's local consumer business.

At Finance Philippines 2026 she delivers the keynote on the future of AI in Philippine banking, addressing how the sector must balance the acceleration of digitisation with a new generation of risks that extend beyond individual institutions and national borders.

Marlon P. Sorongon

Chief Information Security Officer (CISO), Maybank Philippines

Marlon P. Sorongon is the Chief Information Security Officer of Maybank Philippines, a senior leadership role in which he is responsible for the institution's information security strategy, cyber risk management, and resilience frameworks across its Philippine operations.

He oversees defences against an increasingly complex threat landscape — including AI-driven fraud, industrialised scam networks, deepfake-enabled identity attacks, and advanced persistent threats targeting financial institutions in the Philippines and ASEAN.

Marlon brings a practitioner's perspective to the evolving intersection of AI and cybersecurity in banking. At Finance Philippines 2026 he will share insights on how financial institutions can build resilient, trust-preserving security architectures as autonomous AI systems become embedded across banking operations — and what it means for liability, controls, and customer trust when AI agents get it wrong.
