About the Conference

Shaping the Future of Philippine Banking

The Philippine economy enters 2026 on a measured but resilient footing. The World Bank and ADB project GDP growth in the 5.8–6.1% range, supported by household consumption, election-year fiscal stimulus, infrastructure rollout, and continued OFW remittance inflows of around USD 38 billion. For banks, this is the most constructive demand environment since 2019 — but it is also the moment competitive advantage is being recast.

The Philippine banking sector now sits at the intersection of three large transitions running simultaneously: an AI transition moving from co-pilots to autonomous workflows; a money transition in which BSP's Project Agila wholesale CBDC, regulated PHP-linked stablecoins, and tokenised securities are reframing what "bank money" means; and a connectivity transition anchored by ASEAN cross-border QR and the BSP's Open Finance Phase 2 framework.

Finance Philippines 2026 brings together the senior leaders, regulators, and infrastructure providers writing the operating playbook for this new era. Conversations will be biased toward execution: what works, at what unit economics, with what controls, and with what regulator alignment.

International Keynote & Leadership Dialogue

From pilot to P&L: the new operating economics of AI-era Philippine banking

Across global banks, AI has moved from pilots to scaled deployment, with the focus shifting from experimentation to measurable impact. This session explores how AI is reshaping bank economics — from revenue growth and cost-to-serve to risk management and productivity.

  • Where AI productivity is proving real — from KYC, fraud and collections to relationship management
  • How banks should approach agentic AI that can approve, decide or transact on behalf of customers
  • Liability, redress and trust when AI agents get it wrong
  • How bank and central bank leaders are thinking about AI, tokenisation and cross-border connectivity

Panel Discussion

Will agentic AI grow the bank or eat its margin?

The commercial question is no longer whether to deploy agentic AI, but where they create real value, where they erode existing fee pools, and how approval architecture should be redesigned around them.

  • Where agentic workflows are already shifting unit economics — and where they are not
  • Approval architecture: which decisions stay human, which go autonomous, and how is the boundary supervised
  • How to think about deposit, advisory and merchant economics if customers prefer agent-to-agent finance
  • Vendor lock-in, model concentration, and the cost of running multiple frontier models in production
11:15 – 11:30
Solo Presentation

What a production-grade AI banking stack looks like in 2026

Building a bank stack that can run agentic workflows safely at scale demands more than picking a model. It requires a composable core, agent runtime, vector and graph data layers, observability, and an operating model that can put models into production without breaking compliance.

11:30 – 12:10
Panel Discussion

How payment data is rewriting SME credit in the Philippines

Philippine MSMEs remain the country's largest unmet credit opportunity, while payment data is reshaping how lenders assess risk, distribute credit and serve small businesses.

  • How payment-data underwriting is changing default rates, ticket sizes and approval times
  • Closing the merchant acceptance gap in tier-2 and tier-3 cities
  • The economics and controls of embedded SME lending through digital platforms
  • Where banks should partner versus compete with super-apps and digital lenders
12:10 – 12:25
Solo Presentation

AI vs AI: defending the real-time bank against industrialised scams

Network-level AI controls have become essential for defending instant payment systems against industrialised scam operations, mule networks, deepfake-enabled identity attacks and synthetic identity at onboarding.

Panel Discussion

Can AI-augmented RMs finally crack the mass-affluent wealth opportunity?

The Philippine mass-affluent segment is the country's fastest-growing source of wealth-banking revenue, yet it remains under-served by both the private bank model and the retail app model.

  • How AI-augmented RMs change advisory unit economics and the right human/AI handoff design
  • Product shelf: GS, fixed income, FX, multi-currency deposits, structured deposits and tokenised funds
  • Risk profiling and suitability when the advisor is partly an AI agent
  • Customer trust, advisory liability, and the regulator's view on AI-generated advice
14:30 – 15:00
Panel Discussion

Who pays when an AI scams the customer?

Industrial-scale APP scams, mule-account abuse, deepfake-based identity attacks, and offshore-based laundering have turned scam-fraud into a board-level P&L item.

  • Where AI-versus-AI defences have meaningfully reduced loss — and where they have not
  • The reimbursement question: voluntary, regulated or shared — and the implications for growth
  • Mule networks, telco/SIM-swap collaboration, and the AMLC × BSP × NTC enforcement frontier
  • Network-level intelligence sharing: privacy-enhancing analytics and cross-bank consortia

Tokenised deposits, stablecoins and Project Agila move from pilot to production

Digital money is moving from pilot to production, with tokenised deposits, stablecoins and wholesale CBDCs creating new possibilities for payments, treasury and capital markets.

  • Tokenised PHP and USD deposits as treasury and FX rails, from the corporate treasurer's perspective
  • PHPC, regulated PHP stablecoins and the consumer payments use case
  • Project Agila wholesale CBDC: where it sits in the next-generation monetary system
  • Tokenised securities, settlement and the role of the SEC and the PSE

Closing Session

The Philippine banking CEO playbook for 2026–2027

The next 18 to 24 months will define winners and losers across Philippine and ASEAN banking. CEOs face a sharper than usual set of capital, partnership and operating choices.

  • Where to invest in 2026 if cost-to-income has to fall by 300–500 basis points
  • The role of partnerships, M&A and divestiture in a more concentrated digital landscape
  • What ASEAN integration realistically buys a Philippine bank by 2027
  • How regulators, banks and platforms keep the consumer on the rails
