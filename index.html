About the Conference

Overview

The Philippine economy enters 2026 on a measured but resilient footing. The World Bank and ADB project GDP growth in the 5.8–6.1% range, supported by household consumption, election-year fiscal stimulus, infrastructure rollout, and continued OFW remittance inflows of around USD 38 billion. Inflation has stabilised inside the BSP's 2–4% corridor, the policy rate has begun a measured easing cycle, and credit growth has reaccelerated in retail, MSME and consumer segments. For banks, this is the most constructive demand environment since 2019 — but it is also the moment competitive advantage is being recast.

The Philippine banking sector now sits at the intersection of three large transitions running simultaneously. First, an AI transition moving from co-pilots to autonomous workflows in collections, KYC, fraud, advisory and SME credit. Second, a money transition, in which BSP's Project Agila wholesale CBDC, regulated PHP-linked stablecoins, dollar deposit tokens, and tokenised securities are reframing what "bank money" means. Third, a connectivity transition, anchored by ASEAN cross-border QR (which crossed 12.9 million transactions in H1 2025), QR Ph, and the BSP's Open Finance Phase 2 framework.

Finance Philippines 2026 brings together the senior leaders, regulators, and infrastructure providers writing the operating playbook for this new era. Conversations will be biased toward execution: what works, at what unit economics, with what controls, and with what regulator alignment.
