Conference Agenda

Thursday, 13 August 2026  ·  Grand Hyatt Manila, Philippines

Home/Agenda
08:00 – 09:00
Networking

Registration and Morning Networking

09:00 – 10:15
Opening + Keynote

Opening Remarks & Welcome Note

Ana Maria Aboitiz DelgadoCEO, UnionBank; President, BAP

Leadership Dialogue: The new operating economics of AI-era banking

AI in banking has moved beyond experimentation into enterprise-wide deployment. The key question for banks in 2026 is no longer whether to adopt AI, but whether it can materially improve productivity, reduce cost-to-serve, strengthen risk management and create sustainable revenue growth.

  • Where is AI delivering measurable impact on productivity, cost and revenue today?
  • Which banking functions are most ready for autonomous and agentic AI deployment?
  • How should banks manage governance, liability and customer trust when AI makes decisions?
  • What operating model changes will define the next generation of AI-era banks?
Foo Boon PingChair — President & Managing Editor, The Asian Banker
10:15 – 10:45
Break

Networking Break

10:45 – 11:15
Panel Discussion

Can agentic AI unlock the next growth model for banking?

Agentic AI is moving beyond experimentation into real banking operations. As banks scale autonomous capabilities, the focus shifts toward how AI can improve productivity, accelerate decision-making, and create new revenue opportunities.

  • Where is agentic AI creating measurable business value today?
  • Which banking functions are best suited for autonomous workflows?
  • How are banks redesigning human and AI collaboration models?
  • What capabilities will define the next generation of AI-driven banks?
11:15 – 11:30
Solo Presentation

What a production-grade AI banking stack looks like in 2026

Building a bank stack that can run agentic workflows safely at scale requires a composable core, agent runtime, vector and graph data layers, observability, and an operating model that can deploy models without breaking compliance.

11:30 – 12:10
Panel Discussion

How payment data is rewriting SME credit in the Philippines

Philippine MSMEs remain the country's largest unmet credit opportunity, while payment data is reshaping how lenders assess risk, distribute credit and serve small businesses.

  • How payment-data underwriting is changing default rates, ticket sizes and approval times
  • Closing the merchant acceptance gap in tier-2 and tier-3 cities
  • The economics and controls of embedded SME lending through digital platforms
  • Where banks should partner versus compete with super-apps and digital lenders
12:10 – 12:25
Solo Presentation

Can banks turn payment fragmentation into a competitive advantage?

As payment ecosystems become more fragmented, banks must manage transactions seamlessly across real-time payments, cards, cross-border networks, wallets and emerging digital-value rails.

12:25 – 14:00
Networking

Networking Luncheon

14:00 – 14:30
Panel Discussion

Can identity still be trusted in a borderless, real-time financial ecosystem?

As banking becomes embedded across real-time payments, wallets, cross-border rails, and tokenised networks, the core challenge is shifting from fraud prevention to identity trust itself.

  • How are banks moving beyond traditional KYC in a real-time economy?
  • Can continuous authentication replace one-time onboarding?
  • What role should banks, regulators, and platforms play in shared trust infrastructure?
  • How do identity systems adapt to AI agents and delegated payments?
14:30 – 15:00
Panel Discussion

Can AI make mass-affluent wealth banking profitable at scale?

The Philippine mass-affluent segment is growing rapidly, but many banks still struggle to serve it profitably. AI-augmented advisory models are now helping banks scale relationship management and personalise investment engagement.

  • How is AI improving RM productivity and client engagement?
  • What products are driving growth in the mass-affluent segment?
  • Where should banks balance AI automation with human advisory?
  • How are banks addressing trust, suitability and governance in AI-assisted wealth advice?
15:00 – 15:20
Break

Networking Break

15:20 – 15:50
Panel Discussion

Is digital money finally ready for mainstream banking?

Tokenised deposits, stablecoins and wholesale CBDCs are rapidly moving from experimentation into real banking and treasury use cases. As initiatives like Project Agila accelerate, banks, regulators and market infrastructures are redefining how money moves.

  • Where are tokenised deposits and stablecoins creating real business value today?
  • How could Project Agila reshape wholesale payments and settlement?
  • What role will regulated digital money play in treasury, FX and cross-border payments?
  • How are regulators and market infrastructures preparing for tokenised finance?
15:50 – 16:20
Closing Session

What happens when AI becomes embedded across the entire bank?

This closing dialogue brings together AI in Banking experts to look beyond 2026 and map what an AI-native Philippine bank will look like by 2030, and what technology and data leaders should be building today to get there.

  • What will separate AI-native banks from traditional digital banks?
  • What AI talent, skills and operating capabilities will Philippine banks need to compete by 2030?
  • What infrastructure and data foundations must Philippine banks build today?
  • What capabilities and partnerships will matter most in building the future bank?
16:20
End of Day

Closing Remarks
